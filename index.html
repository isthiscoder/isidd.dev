FlashAttention is about memory, not speed

The first time I read the FlashAttention-2 paper, I came away thinking it was a paper about making attention faster. That is the headline — and it is wrong. FlashAttention is not really about attention; it is about how GPUs spend their time. Once you accept that reframe, the rest of the paper stops feeling like a collection of optimizations and starts feeling like a single, elegant consequence of one observation.

What LoRA actually bets on

LoRA is not an approximation of fine-tuning — it is a structural bet about where useful adaptation actually lives. The core claim is that weight updates during fine-tuning have low intrinsic rank, meaning most of the useful change lives in a far smaller subspace than the full weight matrix. The bet is usually right, and the implications go further than most tutorials cover.

Why softmax saturates without the √d_k scaling

The scaling factor in attention is not arbitrary. As dimension grows, the variance of dot products between query and key vectors grows proportionally. Left unscaled, softmax saturates and gradients vanish. The √d_k term exists purely to keep softmax input variance roughly constant regardless of dimension — once derived directly, it stops being a "trick" and becomes the obvious fix to a specific named problem.
